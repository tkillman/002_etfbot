Rivian Automotive, Inc. is an American electric vehicle manufacturer, automotive technology, and outdoor recreation company founded in 2009.

- **Founder:** R. J. Scaringe

- **Area Served:** North America

**Overview**

Rivian produces electric sport utility vehicles (SUVs), pickup trucks on a "skateboard" platform (R1T and R1S models), and electric delivery vans (Rivian EDV). Deliveries of the R1T began in late 2021. The company launched a charging network in the United States in 2022, opening it to other vehicles in 2024. Its production plant is located in Normal, Illinois, with other facilities in various US states, Canada, UK, and Serbia.

**History**

**Beginnings (2009–15):**

- Founded in 2009 by R. J. Scaringe as Mainstream Motors.
- Renamed Rivian Automotive in 2011.
- Initially focused on a sports car prototype (R1) but shifted to electric and autonomous vehicles.

**Setting up Production (2016–20):**

- Acquired a former Mitsubishi Motors manufacturing plant in Normal, Illinois, in 2017 for $16 million.
- In December 2017, revealed its first two products: the R1T (pickup truck) and R1S (SUV).
- Production was scheduled to begin in 2020.

**Shipment of First Models; IPO; Lay-offs and Expansion (2021–24):**

- R1T shipments began in September 2021, making Rivian the first automaker to bring a fully electric pickup to the consumer market.
- In November 2021, Rivian became a public company through an IPO, raising $13.5 billion.
- Ford and Rivian cancelled plans to co-develop an electric vehicle.
- In March 2022, Rivian announced price increases for the R1T and R1S due to component costs, later apologizing for the retroactive impact.
- In July 2022, Rivian announced a workforce reduction of 6%.
- In September 2022, a joint venture with Mercedes-Benz Group to produce electric vans in Europe was announced, but later cancelled.
- October 2022, issued a voluntary recall of 13,000 vehicles due to loose torque bolts.
- June 2023, announced adoption of Tesla's North American Charging System (NACS).
- June 2023, acquired Iternio, the company behind the "A Better Route Planner" (ABRP) EV route-planning app.
- October 2023, ended exclusivity deal with Amazon, allowing Rivian to supply other commercial customers.
- February 2024, announced a 10% reduction of salaried workforce.
- March 2024, unveiled the R2 SUV and R3, with a smaller price tag.

**Partnership with Volkswagen (2024)**

- In June 2024, Volkswagen Group announced an intention to invest up to $5 billion in Rivian, aimed at developing electrical architecture and software technology.

**Vehicles**

- **R1T:** A pickup truck with four electric motors. Battery sizes range from 105 kWh to 180 kWh.
- **R1S:** A sport utility vehicle (SUV) version of the first Rivian platform.
- **Electric Delivery Van (EDV):** An electric van for commercial purposes, primarily designed for and used by Amazon.
- **R2:** A smaller, less-expensive SUV on a new platform, scheduled to launch in early 2026.
- **R3:** An upcoming electric compact SUV.

**EV Charging**

Rivian is developing a network of public charging stations across the United States and Canada. Rivian began installing Waypoint chargers in 2021, but in April 2023 it was announced that the Rivian Adventure Network would be opened up to non-Rivian vehicles. Rivian will also adopt the North American Charging System (NACS) for its vehicles in North America beginning in the 2025 model year.

**Facilities**

- **Irvine, California:** Headquarters focused on vehicle engineering and design.
- **Normal, Illinois:** Manufacturing plant producing vehicle components and performing assembly.
- **Plymouth, Michigan:** Focuses on vehicle engineering, prototyping, supply chain, and accounting.
- **Palo Alto, California:** Focuses on software development and engineering.
- Additional offices in Carson, California, and Woking, England.
- A new $5 billion battery and assembly plant east of Atlanta is on hold.

**Largest Shareholders**

As of December 2023, the largest shareholders were: Amazon, T. Rowe Price International, The Vanguard Group, BlackRock, and Fidelity Investments.

**Collaborations**

Rivian has partnered with Alex Honnold, the Honnold Foundation, Casa Pueblo, Ewan McGregor, Charley Boorman, Yakima, and MAXTRAX.

**Lawsuits**

- In July 2020, Tesla sued Rivian for allegedly stealing proprietary information and poaching employees.
- In March 2021, the Illinois Automobile Dealers Association filed a lawsuit against Rivian and Lucid Motors for direct sales to consumers.
- In November 2021, former VP Laura Schwab filed a suit alleging discrimination and raising concerns about vehicle pricing and safety standards.

Tesla, Inc. is an American multinational automotive and clean energy company. It designs, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles (BEVs), stationary battery energy storage devices, solar panels, solar shingles, and related products/services. Founded in July 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning as Tesla Motors, it was named in honor of Nikola Tesla. Elon Musk led Tesla's initial funding in 2004, becoming chairman and later CEO in 2008.

Tesla's vehicle production began with the Roadster in 2008, followed by the Model S (2012), Model X (2015), Model 3 (2017), Model Y (2020), Semi (2022), and Cybertruck (2023). It is among the world's most valuable companies and the most valuable automaker since July 2020. Tesla briefly reached trillion-dollar status in 2021-2022 and again in 2024. In 2023, it held the largest share of the battery electric vehicle market (19.9%) and was ranked 69th in the Forbes Global 2000.

Tesla has faced lawsuits, government scrutiny, and criticism related to whistleblower retaliation, worker rights violations, safety defects, lack of public relations, and controversial statements from Musk.

## History

### Founding (2003–2004)

Tesla Motors, Inc. was established on July 1, 2003, by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning, who served as CEO and CFO, respectively. Ian Wright joined shortly after. In February 2004, Elon Musk led a $7.5 million Series A funding round, becoming chairman and the largest shareholder. J. B. Straubel joined as CTO in May 2004. All five are recognized as co-founders.

### Roadster (2005–2009)

Elon Musk played an active role, focusing on a strategy of starting with a premium sports car before expanding to mainstream vehicles. Subsequent funding rounds included investments from Valor Equity Partners (2006) and entrepreneurs like Sergey Brin, Larry Page, and Jeff Skoll.

In August 2007, Eberhard was asked to step down as CEO, followed by Tarpenning in January 2008. Michael Marks served as interim CEO before Ze'ev Drori took over, succeeded by Musk in October 2008. Eberhard filed a lawsuit against Musk in June 2009, which was later dismissed.

Roadster production began in 2008. By January 2009, Tesla had raised $187 million and delivered 147 cars. In June 2009, Tesla received a $465 million loan from the U.S. Department of Energy, repaid in May 2013 with interest.

### IPO, Model S, and Model X (2010–2015)

In May 2010, Tesla acquired the NUMMI plant in Fremont, California, from Toyota. The company went public on NASDAQ in June 2010, raising $226 million.

Tesla launched the Model S luxury sedan in June 2012. The Model S received several automotive awards and became the best-selling electric car in Norway (September 2013) and worldwide (2015 and 2016). Tesla joined the NASDAQ-100 in July 2013.

In 2014, Tesla announced Autopilot, a driver-assistance system. Tesla entered the energy storage market in April 2015 with the Powerwall and Powerpack, receiving $800 million in orders within a week. The Model X SUV was launched in September 2015.

### SolarCity and Model 3 (2016–2018)

Tesla acquired SolarCity in November 2016 for $2.6 billion, forming Tesla Energy. In February 2017, Tesla Motors changed its name to Tesla, Inc.

The Model 3 sedan was unveiled in April 2016, receiving over 325,000 reservations in a week. Production challenges, described as "production hell," led to delays. By the end of 2018, the Model 3 became the world's best-selling electric car (2018-2021). In August 2018, Musk announced plans to take Tesla private, which did not materialize and resulted in controversy and a securities fraud charge from the SEC.

### Global expansion and Model Y (2019–present)

From July 2019 to June 2020, Tesla reported four consecutive profitable quarters and was added to the S&P 500. In 2020, its share price increased significantly, and its market capitalization exceeded the next nine largest automakers combined.

The Model Y mid-size crossover SUV was introduced in March 2019, with deliveries starting in March 2020. Tesla expanded its production capacity with Gigafactory Shanghai (2019), Gigafactory Berlin (2020), and Gigafactory Texas (2020).

In March 2023, Tesla announced plans for Gigafactory Mexico, delayed due to potential tariff implications. At the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, Tesla closed the Fremont Factory in March 2020, reopening on May 11, 2020, after a dispute with local authorities. Tesla moved its legal headquarters to Gigafactory Texas in December 2021 but expanded its presence in California with the Megafactory (2022) and a global engineering headquarters in Palo Alto (2023).

In early 2021, Tesla invested $1.5 billion in Bitcoin and briefly accepted it as payment before halting due to environmental concerns. By July 2022, Tesla sold approximately 75% of its Bitcoin holdings. Between May 2023 and February 2024, North American EV manufacturers announced plans to switch to Tesla's North American Charging Standard.

In November 2023, Tesla started shipping the Cybertruck. In April 2024, the company announced layoffs of 10% of its employees and moved its incorporation from Delaware to Texas in June. In October 2024, Tesla unveiled concept versions of the Cybercab and Robovan for a future ridehailing service, the Tesla Network.

In December 2024, a Delaware court rejected Elon Musk's $56 billion pay package, citing improper board approval.

### Available products

- **Model S:** A full-size luxury car with a liftback body style and a dual-motor, all-wheel drive layout. Development of the Model S began before 2007 and deliveries started in June 2012.
- **Model X:** A mid-size luxury crossover SUV offered in 5-, 6- and 7-passenger configurations with either a dual- or trimotor, all-wheel drive layout. The rear passenger doors open vertically with an articulating "falcon-wing" design. A prototype Model X was first shown in February 2012 and deliveries started in September 2015.
- **Model 3:** A mid-size car with a fastback body style and either a dual-motor, all-wheel drive layout or a rear-motor, rear-wheel drive layout. The vehicle was designed to be more affordable than the luxury Model S sedan. A prototype Model 3 was first shown in 2016 and within a week, the company received over 325,000 paid reservations.
- **Model Y:** A mid-size crossover SUV offered in 5- and 7-passenger configurations with a single‐motor, rear-wheel drive or a dual-motor, all-wheel drive layout. The vehicle was designed to be more affordable than the luxury Model X SUV. A prototype Model Y was first shown in March 2019, and deliveries started in March 2020.
- **Tesla Semi:** The Tesla Semi is a Class 8 semi-truck by Tesla, Inc. with a tri-motor, rear-wheel drive layout. Tesla claims that the Semi has approximately three times the power of a typical diesel semi truck, and a range of 500 miles (800 km). Initial deliveries were made to PepsiCo on December 1, 2022.
- **Cybertruck:** A full-sized pickup truck, first announced in November 2019. Three models are offered: rear-wheel drive, dual-motor all-wheel drive, and trimotor all-wheel drive.

### Announced products

- **Roadster (second generation):** Unveiled in 2017, with a purported range of 620 miles (1,000 km) and high-performance specifications. Scheduled for production in 2025.
- **Tesla next-generation vehicle:** An announced battery electric platform expected to be available for delivery in the first half of 2025.
- **Cybercab:** An upcoming two-passenger battery-electric self-driving car.
- **Robovan:** An electric autonomous van planned for future development.

### Discontinued

- **Tesla Roadster:** A two-seater sports car produced from 2008 to 2012.

### Connectivity Services

Tesla cars come with "Standard Connectivity" for navigation. "Premium Connectivity" adds live traffic, satellite maps, internet browsing, and media streaming for a subscription fee.

### Vehicle Servicing

Tesla prioritizes remote diagnosis and repair, dispatching mobile technicians or referring customers to service centers if needed. The company operates retail stores, galleries, service, delivery and body shop locations globally. Tesla aims not to profit from vehicle servicing. In early 2019, annual maintenance requirements were removed, recommending periodic servicing of brake fluid, air conditioning, tires, and air filters instead.

### Charging Services

- **Supercharger network:** Tesla's high-voltage DC fast-charging network, introduced in 2012.
- **Destination charging location network:** Slower chargers at hotels, restaurants, and shopping centers.

### Insurance Services

Tesla offers vehicle insurance in the United States through Tesla Insurance Services, Inc., using vehicle data to offer personalized pricing.

## Energy Products

Tesla Energy develops, builds, sells, and installs solar energy generation systems and battery energy storage products. Products include solar panels, Solar Roof, Solar Inverter, Powerwall, and Megapack. The company operates an online platform for automated power trading, demand forecasting, and product control for large-scale customers.

## Business Strategy

Tesla's strategy involves starting with high-price, low-volume vehicles to reduce battery costs and then offer cheaper, higher-volume cars. Tesla continuously updates its cars' hardware and sells vehicles directly through its website and company-owned stores. Tesla is vertically integrated, developing many components in-house. Tesla generally allows competitors to license its technology to promote sustainable energy adoption.

## Technology

### Batteries

Tesla uses cylindrical and prismatic battery cells sourced from CATL, LG Energy Solution, and Panasonic, and is building capacity to produce its own batteries.

### Software

Tesla uses over-the-air updates to add features or fix problems, enabled by powerful onboard computers.

### Motors

Tesla makes induction motors and internal permanent magnet (IPM) motors with synchronous reluctance motor (SynRM) characteristics.

### North American Charging Standard

The North American Charging Standard (NACS) is an electric vehicle charging connector system developed by Tesla.

### Autopilot and Full Self-Driving

Tesla Autopilot is an advanced driver-assistance system (ADAS) developed by Tesla that amounts to partial vehicle automation.

### Robotics

Tesla uses massive casting machines, known as Giga Presses to create large, single-piece underbodies. The company has been developing a humanoid robot called Optimus since 2022.

## Partners

Tesla has partnerships with Panasonic and long-term contracts for lithium supply. Former partners include Daimler and Toyota.

## Lawsuits and Controversies

Tesla has faced lawsuits and controversies related to sexual harassment, labor disputes, fraud allegations, dealership disputes, intellectual property, environmental violations, property damage, racism, COVID-19 pandemic response, and right to repair.

## Criticism

Tesla has faced criticism related to data privacy, short sellers, delays, vehicle product issues, fires, autopilot crashes, software hacking, phantom braking, and driving range performance.
